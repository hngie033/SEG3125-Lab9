The Motion Picture Association

The Motion Picture Association (MPA), originally known as the Motion Picture Producers and Distributors of America (MPPDA), is an American trade association founded by the major American film studios in 1922 the ensure the viability of the American film industry. Its current members are Paramount Pictures (owned by National Amusements), Sony Pictures (owned by Sony Corporation), Universal Pictures (owned by Comcast), Walt Disney Studios (owned by The Walt Disney Company and parent company of former major studio 20th Century Fox), Warner Bros. (owned by AT&T), and, most recently, Netflix. A Production Code was created in 1930 as a set of moral guildelines for self-censorship, but it was replaced in 1968 by a voluntary film rating system managed by the Classification and Rating Administration (CARA).
The MPA rating system
The following are the current MPA ratings. While any titles on our site are thoroughly reviewed by our moderators, it is still important to review the MPA ratings of any film before showing your children. These ratings often enforced at public venues, but there are no laws preventing children from viewing any content in a private setting. It is your responsibility as a parent to decide what content your children have access to at their age.

What we do
While the MPA rating system is a good starting point for understanding the appropriateness of a film's content, these ratings can often be affected by political or corporate subjectivity. Another thing to consider is that certain aspects of film are judged more loosely or harshly depending on the time that the CARA issued the rating. Our site does not seek to indicate the intensity of these aspects in film. We merely seek to share with fellow parents whether or not violence, sex, or profanity are present within films because whether or not we acknowledge that they occur is not something that can be easily affected by subjectivity. Our titles are reviewed by our moderators, who are parents themselves. As a parent, you are free to contribute by suggesting re-evaluation or submitting a new review to our moderators at the bottom of the homepage.

Our current definitions for reviews are as follows:

• Violence: any harsh fighting or significant display of internals from any living organism.
• Sex: any nudity or depiction, simulation, or explicit description of sexual intercourse.
• Profanity: any explicit language or significant curse.
